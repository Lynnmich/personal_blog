Did You Know?


Cheetah


The cheetah holds the title of being the fastest land animal in the world. With their incredible acceleration and top speeds of up to 113 kilometers per hour, cheetahs can go from 0 to 97 kilometers per hour in just a few seconds.

RHINO


The northern white rhino is one of the most endangered animals on the planet. With only two remaining individuals, both females named Najin and Fatu, who live in the Ol Pejeta Conservancy.

GIRAFFE


Kenya is home to the Masai giraffe, also known as the Maasai giraffe, which is the tallest giraffe species and the largest ruminant on Earth. It can reach a height of up to 18 feet (5.5 meters).

SIMBA


Nairobi National Park was established in 1946, and is the only national park in the world located within a capital city. It is situated just 7 kilometers (4 miles) from the bustling city center of Nairobi.
